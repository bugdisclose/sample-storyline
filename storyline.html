Goal of the adversary:

  1. Data leak
  2. Ransomware
  3. Denial Of Service

Attack Surface & Defenses:

  1. Users
    1. Customers
    2. Employees

      Defense D1b.

      1. Regular security training on social engineering, phishing, vishing, smishing, clean desk policy, etc.
  2. Office premises, which has
    1. Wifi network

      Defense D2a:

      1. Network firewalls
      2. Monitoring & alerting
    2. Endpoints & workstations
      Defense D2b:
      1. JAMF
      2. Disabled USB ports
      3. Cisco Umbrella
      4. OS firewalls
      5. Antivirus
      6. EDR
      7. DLP
      8. Monitoring & alerting
    3. Physical office security
  3. Digital Ecosystem
    1. Public assets
      1. cloud infrastructure
      2. web application
      3. mobile application

      Defense D3a:

      1. Threat hunting (on the internet & within the cloud infrastructure)
      2. Firewalls (WAF, Network, Host, etc.)
      3. Authentication (including MFA), authorization
      4. Security engineering
      5. Regular internal & external VA/PT
        1. Applications are sandboxed
        2. Servers are hardened
        3. Network segregation is ensured
        4. Infra configurations are hardened
      6. Encryption at rest & in transit
      7. Monitoring & alerting & incident response
      8. Disaster recovery
    2. Third party technologies & vendors
      1. product integrations
      2. other technology partners

      D3b. Defense deployed for the above:

      1. IP whitelisting
      2. Third party risk assessments
      3. Compliance certifications, like ISO, SOC2, etc.
    3. Enterprise apps
      1. communication systems, like Slack
      2. Internal finance & HR systems
      3. engineering systems, like JIRA

      D3c. Defense deployed for the above:

      1. Email protection (Mimecast, SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
      2. SSO - Unauthorized access not allowed
      3. All apps up to date & hardened
      4. Monitoring & alerting

Cyber Kill Chains/Attack paths & Defense In Depth

  1. Goal attempted through phishing email
    1. D3c1 disallows phishing email
      1. D1b prevents from malware entry
        1. Browser =>
          • D2b1 prevents browser exploits & malicious extensions
          • D2b3 does not allow visiting a malicious website
        2. Device =>
          • D2b prevents malware execution & subsequent data exfiltration
          • D3a7 prevents data from being exfiltrated
          • D3a4-6 prevents credentials abuse
        3. Device to other potential devices =>
          • D2a prevents lateral movement
    2. Goal attempted through any other enterprise app =>
      1. D3c disallows the entry
  2. Goal attempted through third party compromise or third-party abuse
    1. D3b2 & D3b3 limits third party intentional abuse
    2. D3a1 to D3a7 limits third party abuse through third party compromise
  3. Goal attempted through public assets
    1. Some software security issue (vulnerability) =>
      • D3a1 ensures active threats are immediately handled
      1. D3a2 to D3a7 ensure any residual threat is successively handled
    2. Some credentials found leaked on the open web or dark web =>
      • D3a3 ensures credentials alone are not sufficient to gain access
      • D3a1 & D3a7 ensure threats are immediately handled
      1. Malware lands on infra server =>
        • D3a5 ensures malware will not execute & if at all it is contained within the sandbox & cannot spread on the network or outside
        1. Malware touches database & tries exfiltrating data or encrypting it
          • D3a1 ensures threat is detected
          • D3a7 ensures threat is handled
          1. Malware goes unnoticed & manages to encrypt DB =>
            • D3a8 ensures data can be quickly recovered from regularly taken backups
